Extending the Rotational Library of
1- and 2-Cyanocyclopentadiene

Millimeter-wave Transitions for Astronomical Searches in Hot Cores

Noé Gallifa-Guynot • Laurent Margulès • Brett A. McGuire • Jerôme Loreau

Abstract & Key Findings

Context: 1- and 2-cyanocyclopentadiene are potential precursors to PAHs and have been detected in TMC-1. However, cm-wave data was insufficient for mm-wave searches in warmer sources.

This Work: We extended the analysis to 75–500 GHz.

  • Assigned 7200+ transitions (1-cyano-CPD) and 4000+ (2-cyano-CPD).
  • Reached quantum numbers J=153.
  • Determined sextic and octic distortion constants.
  • Calculated partition functions up to 300 K.

    Experimental Setup

    The study was conducted at the PhLAM laboratory (University of Lille) using the Fast-scan Terahertz Spectrometer (FAST).

    Figure 1: Diagram of the Lille Fast-scan absorption spectrometer (FLASH).

    Pyrolysis Conditions:

    • Precursor: 1H-benzotriazole
    • Temperature: 800°C
    • Pressure: 1.8 × 10-2 mbar
    • Path Length: 2 m quartz cell

    Spectrometer Features:

    • Range Covered: 75–500 GHz (using VDI multipliers).
    • Accuracy: 30–50 kHz (Doppler limited).
    • Technology: Direct Digital Synthesizer (DDS) for fast scanning and precise frequency modulation.

    Analysis & Discussion

    High-Precision Fitting

    We successfully assigned transitions up to J=153 and Ka=50 for 1-cyano-CPD (7219 lines) and Ka=37 for 2-cyano-CPD (4075 lines). The fit achieved microwave RMS values of 47.5 kHz and 44.8 kHz respectively, which are below the estimated peak measurement uncertainties.

    The 3:1 abundance ratio observed in favor of 1-cyano-CPD is consistent with previous studies, explaining the lower number of assigned transitions for the second isomer.

    Importance of High-Frequency Data

    Earlier predictions based on centimeter-wave data diverged significantly (>200 GHz) due to the lack of higher-order centrifugal distortion terms. By extending the range to 500 GHz, we determined sextic and octic parameters that are essential for accurate sub-millimeter predictions.

    Vibrational Contribution

    At 300 K, the vibrational partition function (Qvib) reaches ~5.95, contributing a factor of nearly 6 to the total partition function. Neglecting this in warm environments (like Hot Cores) would lead to a significant underestimation of molecular column densities.

    Data Availability

    The complete line lists, including assigned frequencies and intensities for both 1- and 2-cyanocyclopentadiene, are available in the Lille Spectroscopic Database (LSD).
